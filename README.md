# Portfolio Reflection

## 1. How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program?
User needs are interpreted by gathering requirements, analyzing feedback, and translating them into features or requirements. Creating user stories helps because they break down features into simple, understandable goals from the user’s perspective. This makes development more focused and ensures the final product meets real user expectations.

## 2. How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate in the future?
I approach development by breaking work into smaller tasks, planning iterations, and continuously improving the product. Agile processes like Scrum, sprint planning, and retrospectives help keep development flexible and organized. In future work, I want to continue using iterative development, frequent feedback loops, and regular testing to improve quality and efficiency.

## 3. What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
A good team member communicates clearly, contributes consistently, and is open to feedback. It also means being reliable, helping solve problems collaboratively, and supporting team goals rather than individual goals.
